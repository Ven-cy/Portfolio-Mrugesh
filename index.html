Ethical AI & System Design

Building systems that make automated decisions, especially in AI, carries an immense ethical weight. My design philosophy is grounded in creating systems that are not only powerful but also fair, transparent, and secure.

Case Study: Model Bias in Fraud Detection

Scenario: In developing VeriSentry, our initial model showed a higher false-positive rate for transactions from specific geographic regions.
Analysis & Reflection: I mandated the integration of an "AI Fairness 360" toolkit into our MLOps pipeline. We now actively monitor and report on model fairness metrics (e.g., disparate impact) and use techniques like adversarial debiasing to mitigate bias before a model is promoted to production.

Global Perspective: Data Sovereignty

Influence: Deploying the SaaS platform globally required us to handle data privacy laws like GDPR (EU) and CCPA (California).
Analysis & Reflection: This forced us to architect our system for data sovereignty from day one. We designed a multi-region architecture on AWS, ensuring that user data could be pinned to specific geographic regions (e.g., EU data stays in the Frankfurt region).
